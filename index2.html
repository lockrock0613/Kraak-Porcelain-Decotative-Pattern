Kraak Porcelain Decotative Pattern Restoration Using Generative AI:

a Piolt Study

Yujie Ren , Haotian Lu , Chi Liu ,

Paper GitHub Dataset

Abstract

This study explores the generation and restoration of Kraak porcelain decorative patterns using a Stable Diffusion model enhanced with Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA) and ControlNet. Kraak porcelain, a historic blue-and-white ceramic traded along the maritime Silk Road, features intricate radial "open light" patterns that blend Chinese and Islamic artistic styles. Traditional methods struggle to systematically decode pattern generation rules, leading to limited innovation. The authors constructed a multi-dimensional cultural database of 709 Kraak porcelain images to train the model by stable diffusion method. The model was fine-tuned using LoRA to adapt to small-sample data and integrated with ControlNet to impose structural constraints via edge maps. Experiments demonstrated that combining LoRA and ControlNet improved structural control over open light patterns and secondary motifs, validated by metrics like SSIMand PSNR. While the method enhanced pattern consistency, challenges remained in detail accuracy and seamless restoration of damaged patterns. Future work aims to refine incremental learning and interdisciplinary collaboration to bridge technical and cultural interpretation gaps. This approach offers a framework for digital preservation and innovative regeneration of cultural heritage.
